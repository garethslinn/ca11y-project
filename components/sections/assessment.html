4. Assessment and Testing

4.1 Non-Discriminatory Testing Methods

  • 4.1.1 Accessible Formats

    • Design tests that are compatible with screen readers, keyboard navigation, and other assistive technologies.

  • 4.1.2 Time Flexibility

    • Offer untimed or extended-time tests to accommodate candidates with slower processing speeds or anxiety.

  • 4.1.3 Clear Instructions

    • Provide explicit, step-by-step instructions and examples to ensure candidates understand the test requirements.

  • 4.1.4 Stress-Free Environment

    • Allow candidates to complete assessments in a calm and comfortable setting, either remotely or in-person.

4.2 Alternatives to Traditional Assessment Methods

  • 4.2.1 Project-Based Assessments

    • Replace live coding tests or timed exercises with project-based assessments that candidates can complete over several days.

  • 4.2.2 Portfolios and Case Studies

    • Evaluate a candidateâ€™s skills through a portfolio review or discussion of past work rather than high-pressure testing scenarios.

  • 4.2.3 Collaborative Challenges

    • Introduce team-based exercises or pair-programming sessions, which simulate real-world problem-solving in a supportive environment.

  • 4.2.4 Iterative Feedback

    • Provide feedback during assessments, allowing candidates to adjust their approach and demonstrate their capabilities in a less rigid context.

By implementing these inclusive practices, organisations can create a fair and supportive recruitment experience, ensuring that all candidates regardless of cognitive or neurological conditions are given equal opportunities to succeed.
