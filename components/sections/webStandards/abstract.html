Overview

This document introduces the Cognitive Accessibility Web Standards (CAWS), a comprehensive framework aimed at ensuring web experiences are inclusive and accessible for individuals with cognitive and psychological conditions. As the digital landscape becomes increasingly integral to daily life, it is essential for designers, developers, testers, and UX professionals to address the unique challenges faced by this often-overlooked user group. The guidelines outlined in this document provide actionable strategies to create user interfaces that reduce cognitive load, alleviate anxiety, and enhance usability for everyone, regardless of their cognitive or psychological profile. By adopting these standards, teams can foster inclusivity, improve user satisfaction, and align with emerging accessibility regulations.

This guide is tailored for:

  • Developers: Crafting web experiences that prioritize cognitive accessibility.
  • Designers: Creating visually and functionally inclusive interfaces.
  • UX Professionals: Understanding and addressing the needs of diverse user groups.
  • Testers: Ensuring compliance with accessibility standards during QA.
  • Product Owners and Managers: Embedding accessibility principles into project goals and workflows.

CAWS serve as a vital resource for anyone involved in the web development lifecycle, equipping teams with the tools and insights needed to make digital spaces more inclusive for individuals with cognitive and psychological conditions.
