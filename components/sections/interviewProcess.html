3. Interview Process

This section provides practical tools for HR professionals and hiring managers to create application processes that are equitable and accessible, aligning with the principles outlined in this guide.

3.1 Conducting Inclusive Interviews

  • 3.1.1 Respect and Empathy

    • Begin with a warm introduction, explaining the structure and purpose of the interview.
    • Example: "We want you to feel comfortable, so we'll start with introductions, then move through some questions about your experience and skills."

  • 3.1.2 Clarify Expectations

    • Clearly outline what you are looking for in responses, avoiding ambiguity.
    • Example: "For this question, we're looking for examples of how you've solved a problem under pressure."

  • 3.1.3 Avoid Surprise Questions

    • Eliminate unexpected or "gotcha" questions, which can increase anxiety and disadvantage neurodivergent candidates.

  • 3.1.4 Provide Feedback Opportunities

    • Allow candidates to clarify or revisit their answers if they feel they haven't communicated their thoughts effectively.

3.2 Environment and Accessibility

  • 3.2.1 Comfortable Environment

    • Choose a quiet, well-lit, and distraction-free setting for the interview.
    • For virtual interviews, ensure both parties have stable internet connections and accessible technology.

  • 3.2.2 Minimal Stimulation

    • Avoid overly stimulating environments, which may be overwhelming for candidates with sensory sensitivities.

  • 3.2.3 Accessibility Features

    • For in-person interviews, ensure physical spaces are wheelchair-accessible and provide assistive devices like hearing loops or notetaking aids if needed.

3.3 Panel Training

  • 3.3.1 Bias Awareness

    • Train interviewers to recognise and mitigate unconscious biases, ensuring candidates are evaluated based on skills and qualifications rather than stereotypes.

  • 3.3.2 Understanding Neurodivergence

    • Educate panel members about common challenges faced by neurodivergent individuals, such as difficulty with eye contact or processing verbal instructions.

  • 3.3.3 Inclusive Questioning

    • Encourage interviewers to use open-ended yet structured questions and to rephrase or clarify if a candidate appears confused.

  • 3.3.4 Feedback and Reflection

    • After interviews, facilitate discussions among panel members to ensure fair and balanced evaluations of candidates.
