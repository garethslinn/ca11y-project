1. Job Specification and Announcement

Creating an inclusive recruitment process begins with thoughtfully crafting job specifications and announcements. This section provides guidance on developing clear, unbiased job descriptions and effectively communicating your organisation's commitment to accessibility and inclusivity.

1.1 Creating Inclusive Job Descriptions

  • 1.1.1 Use Clear and Simple Language

    • Avoid jargon, acronyms, and overly technical terms that may be confusing.
    • Ensure that responsibilities and expectations are articulated in straightforward terms.

  • 1.1.2 Eliminate confusing or irrelevant buzz words

    • Use job titles that accurately reflect the role without unnecessary hierarchy or exclusivity (e.g., "Software Developer" instead of "Senior Ninja Software Developer" or “Rockstar Engineer”).

  • 1.1.3 Clearly Define Essential vs. Desirable Criteria

    • Use familiar language such as “Required Skills” or “Required Experience” or “Nice-to-have.
    • Encourage candidates to apply even if they meet most, but not all, of the criteria.

  • 1.1.5 Focus on Skills and Competencies

    • Emphasise the skills and competencies required to perform the job successfully rather than focusing solely on formal qualifications.
    • Many experienced candidates may not possess formal qualifications but have years or decades of experience.

  • 1.1.6 Promote Flexible Working Arrangements

    • If applicable, highlight options for remote work, flexible hours, or hybrid models to attract a diverse range of candidates.

1.2 Additional Elements for Inclusive Job Announcements

  • 1.2.1 Use of Multiple Channels for Job Postings

    • Advertise job openings on platforms that reach diverse audiences, including disability-focused job boards and community organisations.
    • Ensure job sites are web accessible.

  • 1.2.2 Provide Detailed Information About the Role

    • Offer comprehensive details about the job responsibilities, team structure, and company culture to help candidates assess their fit.
    • Example: "Our team values collaboration and innovation, and we encourage open communication and continuous improvement."

  • 1.2.3 Transparent Salary and Compensation Information

    • Whenever possible, include salary ranges and information about compensation packages to promote transparency and reduce barriers for applicants.
    • Example: "The salary range for this position is £70,000 - £90,000, depending on experience and qualifications."

  • 1.2.4 Clear Application Instructions

    • Provide step-by-step instructions on how to apply, including any required documents or specific formats to submit applications.
    • Example: "To apply, please submit your CV / resume and a cover letter detailing your relevant experience through our online portal."
