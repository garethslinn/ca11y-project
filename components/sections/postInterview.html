5. Post-Interview

5.1 Feedback and Communication

  • 5.1.1 Timely Responses

    • Notify candidates of the outcome promptly, regardless of whether they were successful, to reduce anxiety and uncertainty.

  • 5.1.2 Constructive Feedback

    • Provide actionable and respectful feedback that highlights strengths and areas for improvement.
    • Example: "Your problem-solving skills stood out, and we recommend expanding on how you apply these skills in collaborative settings."

  • 5.1.3 Personalised Communication

    • Tailor feedback to the individual, acknowledging their effort and unique contributions.
    • Avoid generic or impersonal messages.

  • 5.1.4 Accessibility in Feedback

    • Ensure feedback is provided in an accessible format, such as email, audio recordings, or phone calls, based on the candidate's preferences.

5.2 Offer and Onboarding

  • 5.2.1 Inclusive Offer Letters

    • Use clear and accessible language in offer letters, outlining key details such as salary, start date, and benefits.
    • Offer the letter in alternative formats if needed.

  • 5.2.2 Onboarding Accommodations

    • Discuss and arrange accommodations during onboarding, such as assistive technology, adjusted work hours, or a workplace buddy system.

  • 5.2.3 Comprehensive Orientation

    • Provide a detailed orientation process, including visual guides, checklists, or one-on-one sessions, to help new hires acclimate comfortably.

  • 5.2.4 Ongoing Support

    • Assign a point of contact for new hires to address questions and provide additional support as they transition into their role.
